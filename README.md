# Miles Sound System SDK 9.3b (2012'12'12) — PC-Windows
Published by RAD Game Tools (now part of Epic Games)

Version 9.3b, from 2012'12'12, preserved and maintained by Archivo Federal.

Archived and published for educational use, preservation, and community support.

The version 9.3 represents one of last legacy release of Miles Sound System before its phase-out. The Archivo Federal has archived and published this version for educational, preservation, and modding community purposes.

### Project goals

- Education: Provide a real-world case study in audio middleware design and legacy software engineering.
- Preservation: Ensure the survival and accessibility of historically significant software for future generations.
- Modding Support: Empower community modders to restore or enhance audio in classic games.
- Collaboration: Invite a community of enthusiasts, educators, and developers to contribute to ongoing preservation efforts.

### What’s included

This repository contains:

## About Miles Sound System

The Miles Sound System (MSS) is one of the most influential audio middleware libraries in the history of PC and console gaming. Originally developed by John Miles, then embraced by RAD Game Tools, MSS powered audio in thousands of commercial titles across DOS, Windows, PlayStation, Xbox, Dreamcast, GameCube, and more. If you have ever played a game between 1995 and 2010, there is a good chance you have "heard" the work of Miles.

Miles Sound System was used in over 7,000 titles, ranging from AAA franchises to indie classics. Understanding how MSS functioned gives insight into:

- Middleware engineering practices from the late ’90s and early 2000s
- Cross-platform audio abstraction layers
- The evolution of audio codecs, 3D sound, and streaming
- Licensing and binary distribution models
- Challenges in preserving proprietary software
